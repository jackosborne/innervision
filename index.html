Interior Design

One of the Fundamental elements required in Hospitality Design is Atmosphere and Ambiance making all the difference to selling the product and getting the customers in the door. But the Interior Design has the responsibility of creating the mood of the space which determines and improves the experience.

Space Planning

Good Space Planning and clever use of Scale allows the guest to feel comfortable with the space allowing them not to feel that they can dine in comfort. Good circulation and flow through the space also assists the staff providing Table Service from bumping into each other or guests moving about the restaurant when it comes down to Buffet Service.

Lighting

No one should need to bring their IPhone out to light up the menu to read them. The correct principle of good lighting is fundamental

Kitchen to Table

Understanding how food is presented and served to the customer can also make a big difference to how the guest feels that they have been treated with care and consideration. The last thing one wants to see is the customer feeling that they did not get provided with Service if they are being charged money or something that is not good. Staff and how the table is set up plays a vital part of the guest experience. When good, the customer will say and recommend. When bad they simply will not come back and that is the last thing you want.

There are many different levels of Table Set UP. From fine dining with all the appropriate Glass wear / Stem wear, Flat wear, Cutlery to be presented at each course or just a simple basic table setting.

If one was lucky to be invited to Buckingham Palace for a State Dinner one would see that every alignment of Plate / Glass / Fork and Spoon have been carefully measured by rulers to ensure that each place setting is the same as the person adjacent to you. I am not saying that for Hospitality one has to go to these measures but with experience guidance from InnerVision Design it can make all the first impressions the difference. “The Devil is in the detail”

Display Kitchens

Such as seen for Buffet Service

Establishing what you want the customer to see from a Display Kitchen needs to be thought out before you can start planning a Kitchen. types of food that will be prepared and served from an open space. Number of chef’s working there also needs to be considered as the last thing you want to see is them all falling over themselves in a badly designed Kitchen.

Knowing and fully understanding how a Buffet Counter should work is also a very specialist subject. As some of the Display Kitchens are providing freshly cooked food or replenishing food to a Buffet Counter line.

First impressions on presentation of the Food is extremely important as this provides the customer with an insight of what they can select to eat. The need for the right Kitchen Equipment fitted in the Interior Designer’s Counters also plays a vital part of the Buffet Set Up. Only through experience of what both parties need can make the whole Buffet flow with ease.

Bar Service

Providing drinks for a Bar is not all just about having a line of alcoholic bottles on a Back Gantry and Front Counter serving them over to the guest. with many different types of Drinks available from a Bar.

The Set Up needs to work for the best efficiency whether it be from making a cocktail with multiple ingredients to having cut fruit on hand to decorate the glass.

Various temperatures come into this factor too from chilled white wine to red wine that has been decanted etc. Often or not the Coffee Beverage Station is located at the Bar so storage for everything that comes with that needs to be on hand in storage. Not to mention the impression Coffee Machine itself which usually is a centre piece feature on the back bar.

Health & Hygiene

It does not need to be said that Health and Hygiene plays a very big part of Restaurant , Bar and Buffet requirements. Without keeping to the guidelines food can go dangerously wrong and be contaminated very quickly.

Even the Space Planning to a Restaurant has to be considered when it comes to newly cooked / fresh food coming out and waste and dirty dishes being transported to the Back of House Dish Washing Areas.

Correct temperature control is extremely critical to providing the Fridges / Chillers / Wine Cabinets / Display Refrigeration with their correct settings.
